AI-Powered Interview Preparation Platform

Building interview-readiness infrastructure using AI and real interview data for MBA students.

Timeline Nov 2025 – Jan 2026
Role Product Strategy, Research & MVP Scope
Type Team Project · GLIM Chennai
Context PGPM — Product Management Specialisation

Worked across product strategy, design, and AI workflow decisions, owning end-to-end product thinking from problem discovery to MVP execution. Contributed to user research and insight synthesis, and defined MVP scope, priorities, and trade-offs.

01 · Problem Space

A structurally broken preparation ecosystem

Candidates invest significant time and effort in interview preparation through mock interviews, online content, alumni guidance, and AI tools. Despite this, many still walk into interviews underprepared and uncertain — not due to lack of motivation or effort, but because the current interview preparation ecosystem is structurally broken.

01

Practice is Slot-Based & Limited

Preparation depends on availability of seniors, alumni, or paid mentors — creating scheduling constraints and inconsistent access.

02

Feedback is Generic & Non-Actionable

Most tools provide high-level feedback like "be more confident" without explaining what went wrong or how to improve.

03

Resume Screening Failures Lack Clarity

Candidates filtered during resume screening receive no structured explanation on role fit, keyword alignment, or expectations.

04

Learning Resets Every Batch

Real interview experiences and insights are lost after each cohort graduates. Each new batch starts from scratch.

02 · Key Insight

It's an infrastructure problem, not a content problem

Candidates do not need more articles, videos, or generic mock questions. They need a system that provides knowledge continuity across cohorts, always-available practice, role- and company-specific realism, and clear, actionable feedback loops.

How might we enable candidates to prepare effectively for company- and role-specific interviews using real interview experiences, while ensuring 24×7 availability, actionable feedback, and continuity across cohorts?

04 · Product Vision & Principles

A student-owned, AI-powered readiness system

Build a system that enables candidates to prepare effectively by being compounding across cohorts, always available, role- and company-specific, and feedback-driven.

🎯

Precision over Generic Prep

Focus on role- and company-specific readiness instead of broad interview content.

💡

Feedback over Surface Scores

Prioritize actionable insights that change behavior, not just numerical ratings.

⏰

Availability over Scheduling

Design for preparation that works anytime, not only when humans are available.

🏗️

Infrastructure over Content

Build a system that compounds learning rather than a static collection of materials.

06 · Feature Deep Dives

Core product experiences

AI Mock Interviews

Problem: Lack of scalable and realistic interview practice

AI-led video interviews with immediate structured feedback — available 24×7 without scheduling constraints. Candidates select a target role and company; the system generates contextually relevant questions and evaluates responses on structure, content, and communication clarity. Post-interview, a detailed scorecard breaks down performance per question.

Resume & JD Analyzer

Problem: Resume rejections without clarity on root cause

Candidates upload their resume and a target job description. The system highlights gaps in keyword alignment, role-fit, and experience framing — providing a structured breakdown of what to strengthen and why. Tracks improvement over iterations.

Learning Hub & Question Repository

Problem: Practicing generic questions unrelated to target roles

A repository of real interview questions tagged by company and role, contributed by students across cohorts. Ends the cycle of each batch starting from scratch. Coupled with curated learning resources relevant to specific domains and companies.

Daily Challenges

Problem: Inconsistent preparation over long placement cycles

Bite-sized daily questions — guesstimates, case snippets, or behavioral prompts — with streak tracking to drive habit formation. Keeps candidates in active preparation mode even between intensive mock sessions.

Feedback Dashboard

Problem: No visibility into preparation progress over time

A central view of all practice activity — mock interview scores, resume analyzer history, daily challenge streaks, and readiness trends. Enables candidates and institutions to track improvement trajectories over the placement season.

07 · Feedback Framework

Structured improvement loops, not surface scores

The core differentiator of the platform is how it delivers feedback. Rather than a single score, each mock interview surfaces a multi-dimensional evaluation:

  • Content Quality — relevance, accuracy, and depth of the answer
  • Structure — STAR / framework usage, logical flow
  • Role Alignment — how well the response maps to the target role and company
  • Communication Clarity — conciseness, confidence, and delivery
  • Improvement Suggestions — specific, actionable next steps per question

Feedback is benchmarked against historical responses for the same role, so candidates understand not just where they stand but what "good" looks like in context.

10 · Go-To-Market Strategy

Phased rollout starting from a closed cohort

The objective is to validate usefulness, drive habitual usage, and establish the platform as core interview preparation infrastructure — not an optional tool.

  • 1

    Pilot — Product Validation

    Launch with one graduating batch to observe usage patterns, refine feedback quality, and identify drop-offs. Entry point: closed cohort at GLIM Chennai.

  • 2

    Institutional Adoption — Scale Depth

    Expand to all batches and integrate into official interview preparation workflows. Introduce institution-level readiness insights and reporting.

  • 3

    Expansion — Scale Width

    Replicate the model across other institutions using the knowledge-compounding infrastructure built in Phase 1–2.

Business Model

Primary: Institution-level subscription providing platform access for all students, a central interview knowledge base, and usage & readiness insights for the institution.

Secondary: Optional student premium tier — advanced mock interviews, deeper feedback views, priority access to new features.

This structure lowers acquisition cost through institutional entry, guarantees baseline usage, and ensures knowledge continuity across cohorts.

11 · Risks & Mitigation

Anticipated challenges and how we address them

Risk

Low Interview Data Contribution

Platform depends on real interview data being submitted by users.

→ Incentives, anonymity, frictionless submission flow

Risk

Low Trust in AI Feedback

Candidates may doubt AI accuracy compared to human reviewers.

→ Benchmark feedback against human-reviewed samples

Risk

Low Sustained Engagement

Prep fatigue during intensive placement seasons is a real risk.

→ Daily challenges and visible progress tracking

Risk

Over-Reliance on AI

AI alone doesn't fully simulate real interview pressure.

→ Hybrid AI + human positioning from the start

14 · Final Takeaway

What this project reflects

This project reflects how I approach 0→1 product building: problem-first thinking, user-validated decisions, disciplined MVP scoping, and systems that scale beyond individual users. The hardest part wasn't the AI — it was resisting the temptation to build everything and instead focusing relentlessly on the one loop that mattered most: candidates getting better between each practice session.

Interview preparation is not a content problem. It is an infrastructure problem. We built infrastructure.
